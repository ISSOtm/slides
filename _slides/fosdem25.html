Pixel art

  • Dedicated pixel art tools (Aseprite, Piskel...)
  • Monochrome GB art (4 shades of gray, dithering for nuance)
  • GBC art (unusual colour restrictions)
ğŸ¶ You can use practically any graphics editor to draw things one pixel at a time. But the Game Boy has more restrictions than that. â­ï¸

Dedicated editors for pixel art have useful features, like working with limited color palettes or animating a frame at a time. â­ï¸

The original Game Boy could show exactly four shades its famous â€œpea soup greenâ€. But even then, you can use dithering -- mixing colors over space, or even â­ï¸ over time -- to look like you have more. â­ï¸

The Game Boy Color supported 32 thousand whole colors -- but still with its own limits around how they're divided into palettes and tiles. But once again, you can use artistic and programming trickery to create "high-color" cutscenes or demos.
